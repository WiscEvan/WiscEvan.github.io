Computional Biology in the Marine Environment

My current research involves understanding conditional metabolism in marine endosymbiotic bacteria. This has allowed me to ask questions relating to how biosynthetic gene clusters (BGCs) are maintained and how they may develop across different species. The relationship between conditional metabolism and host-associated bacteria is generally not well understood. To understand possible evolutionary processes involved, I incorporate biological, informatic and analytical chemistry techniques to uncover selective pressures in a particular biological niche. Currently, my research is leading me to explore how one might identify and compare homologous BGCs to access answers surrounding these questions, specifically, regarding evolution and horizontal gene transfer.

Code

My bioinformatics workflows have routinely sharpened my abilities to operate within python, bash, and R scripting environments. My tasks include employing an array of DNA annotation and alignment tools to annotate biosynthetic gene clusters in metagenome-assembled genomes. With a diligence for reproducibility, I combine these tasks to build custom data pipelines. My data exploration utilizes Adobe Illustrator, and a variety of Rstudio and Python libraries to measure and visualize complex biological systems. I have used docker,to suit both reproducibility and high throughput computing environments to handle big data in a flexible, time-effective and computationally efficient manner.
